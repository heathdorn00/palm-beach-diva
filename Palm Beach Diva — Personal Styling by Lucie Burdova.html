Hourglass
Balanced & Defined

Shoulders and hips are similar in width with a noticeably narrower waist. The most balanced silhouette — fitted styles work beautifully.

✓ Wrap dresses
✓ Fitted silhouettes

Hips and thighs are the widest point. Balance the silhouette by drawing attention upward with statement tops and structured shoulders.

✓ Off-shoulder tops

✓ Bold prints on top

✓ V-necklines

Style Guide

What to Wear in Palm Beach: A Local's Guide to the Season

If you've ever been on Palm Beach island during season — November through April — you know that dressing here is an art form. After years on the island's social calendar, here's my honest insider guide.

Lucie Burdova · March 2026

Worth Avenue: Polished Casual

The dress code on Worth Avenue is what I call "polished casual." You're surrounded by Cartier and Hermès — you want to look like you belong. My go-to: well-fitted linen, a quality tote, and most importantly a great hat. The sun is no joke, and a chic wide-brim hat protects your skin and makes every outfit look intentional. Shoes: block heels. You'll be walking cobblestone — stilettos are a mistake you only make once.

Sunday Polo

The dress code is garden party meets equestrian chic. Floral midi dresses, pastel blazers, a wide-brim hat, and wedges — heels sink into the grass during divot stomping. Colors: coral, mint, powder blue, blush. Anything too dark or casual is wrong energy.

Charity Galas

Go bold with color or architectural with silhouette. Palm Beach women do not blend in. A cobalt column gown, a hot pink off-the-shoulder, a structured emerald ballgown — these are the looks that get remembered. Jewelry: gold, layered, chandelier earrings. More is more.

The 5 Things You Actually Need

  • A wide-brim hat — sun protection that looks intentional
  • One bold-color dress — your polo and gala moment
  • A quality linen or silk blouse — the Worth Avenue workhorse
  • Strappy wedges or block heels — the terrain is varied
  • Oversized gold jewelry — because accessories are the whole point

Season runs fast. Dress intentionally — in Palm Beach, the dress code is always a suggestion to do more.

Personal Style

How I Developed My Style Traveling 90+ Countries

I was born in Prague — a city with centuries of architecture and a very specific kind of elegant restraint. Then I left. Monte Carlo. Milan. Paris. Palm Beach. In every country, I watched women. How they dressed. What they chose to wear. I took notes.

Lucie Burdova · February 2026

Monte Carlo: Invest in What Photographs

Monaco women invest heavily in a few statement pieces — a spectacular bag, exquisite heels, one jaw-dropping dress — and build everything else simply. They don't compete on quantity. They compete on impact. Identify your three statement pieces and keep everything else clean.

Italian Women: Fit Is Non-Negotiable

Italian women will spend €50 on a blouse and €200 tailoring it. This seems strange to Americans who do the opposite. But when something genuinely fits your body — not the size chart — it transforms how you carry yourself. Budget for alterations. A perfectly tailored mid-range piece beats an ill-fitting designer piece every time.

French Women: Own Your Signature

French women are not chasing trends. They have a version of themselves they've decided to be, and they dress that way every day. I watched a woman in Paris wear the same three combinations all week. She looked iconic every day because she'd found her thing and committed completely. Stop trying to be fashionable. Start trying to be recognizable.

Palm Beach: Joy Is a Style

Color. Print. Jewelry stacked on jewelry. Hats at lunch. A woman in sequins at 2pm on Worth Avenue. She looked incredible — because she was completely unapologetic. Palm Beach women have decided that dressing beautifully is an act of joy, not vanity.

"Style is not about spending money. It is about paying attention."

Capsule Wardrobe

The 5 Pieces Every Woman Needs for a Palm Beach Capsule Wardrobe

The best-dressed women on the island always look like they've made an effort, but not a tortured effort. That's not luck. That's a capsule wardrobe. Here are the five pieces that make it work.

Lucie Burdova · January 2026

1. The Statement Hat

Not a baseball cap — a hat that is itself an outfit. Wide-brimmed, structured, completely intentional. The practical case is strong (Florida sun is relentless) but the real reason: a great hat elevates everything beneath it. A simple sundress becomes an occasion. Our Palm Beach Diva hats were designed exactly for this moment.

2. One White Linen Piece

White linen is the fabric of Palm Beach. It breathes, it photographs beautifully, and it reads as effortlessly expensive. A loose midi dress or an oversized shirt — either works. The wrinkles are part of it. Don't fight them. In Palm Beach, rumpled linen reads as "I just came from the boat."

3. A Bold Color Dress

Hot pink. Cobalt blue. Emerald. The color that reads from across the room and makes people turn. This is your polo dress, your gala dress, your "I'm going to Café L'Europe" dress. Look for something with movement — a wrap, an off-shoulder, a flutter sleeve.

4. Statement Sunglasses

In Palm Beach, sunglasses are not eye protection. They're a personality. The oversized square in tortoiseshell. The cat-eye in white acetate. The aviator with a pink lens. (Our logo is literally crown-and-aviators — we understand this assignment.) Spend a little more than you think you should — cheap frames are visible at twenty feet.

5. Gold Jewelry — Layers of It

Palm Beach is a gold town. The specific Palm Beach way: layered, mixed, slightly maximalist. Add one more piece after you think you're done. This is correct behavior. A good chain, oversized earrings, and a cuff bracelet will work in every combination for every occasion.

"Palm Beach style is not about having more. It's about choosing better."
